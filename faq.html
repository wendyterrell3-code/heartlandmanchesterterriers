Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Standard Manchester Terrier like to live with?

Standard Manchester Terriers are intelligent, loyal, and observant dogs that form strong bonds with their people. They are active without being frantic and do best in homes that value structure, engagement, and companionship. While affectionate, they are typically thoughtful rather than indiscriminately social and appreciate clear expectations.

Do you health test your dogs?

Yes. All breeding dogs at Heartland Manchester Terriers complete comprehensive health testing appropriate for the breed, with results registered through OFA where applicable. Testing includes genetic screening and clinical evaluations, with additional testing completed as age and circumstance allow. Transparency and long-term health are priorities in every breeding decision.

How often do you have litters?

Heartland Manchester Terriers is a small, purpose-driven program. Litters are planned selectively and infrequently, with each breeding evaluated individually. Quality, health, and long-term responsibility are prioritized over volume or availability.

How are puppies raised?

Puppies are raised in a home environment with early socialization and age-appropriate enrichment. Care is taken to support confidence, resilience, and stability during critical developmental periods. Puppies are introduced to household sounds, handling, and structured routines before going to their new homes.

How do you decide puppy placement?

Placement decisions are made thoughtfully, considering temperament, structure, and the individual goals of each home. While preferences are taken into account, the priority is matching each puppy to the environment where it is most likely to thrive. This approach supports long-term success for both the puppy and the family.

Are puppies sold with full registration?

Pet and companion puppies are sold with limited registration. Full registration may be considered after spay or neuter is completed within the timeframe outlined in the purchase contract. Any breeding or co-ownership arrangements are discussed individually and in advance.

What is your spay/neuter policy?

Spay or neuter timing is based on the health and development of the individual dog and is outlined in the purchase contract. The goal is to support long-term structural and physical well-being while preventing unplanned or inappropriate breeding.

Do you offer a health guarantee?

Yes. Puppies are sold with a written health guarantee that is outlined in the purchase contract. This guarantee reflects responsible breeding practices and reasonable expectations, with the long-term welfare of the dog as the primary focus.

What happens if an owner can no longer keep their dog?

Any dog bred by Heartland Manchester Terriers must be returned to us or placed with our involvement if the owner can no longer keep the dog. This lifetime responsibility ensures that no dog produced by our program is ever left without support.
